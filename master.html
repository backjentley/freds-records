Ad Strategy Reference
Ads amplify what's already converting. Prove organic conversion rate first (minimum 2 weeks of organic data). Then activate paid. A bad store with ad spend burns money faster.
Order of ad activation. 1) Organic email drops → 2) Google Shopping (high intent titles) → 3) Meta retargeting (site visitors + email list) → 4) Meta cold (diaspora) → 5) TikTok (brand awareness only)
Google Shopping ROAS: 5–10x. Best ROI channel. CPC: $0.40–$1.50 CAD. Target: specific album titles Murray knows are moving (Rumours, Bowie, Taylor Swift, Kendrick). Activate first.
Meta diaspora ROAS: 4–8x. Target: "lived in Newfoundland," now in Calgary, Edmonton, Toronto, Ottawa. Best ad creative: 15-sec Reel of the store, the bins, Tony, ending on fredsrecords.com. Emotional beats product catalog.
Break-even ROAS on used vinyl: 1.1–1.3x. 75–90% gross margins. Almost impossible to run a losing used vinyl ad campaign. New vinyl needs 2.86x+ to break even — be selective with new titles.
Seasonal peaks. Nov–Dec: 2–3x normal conversion (gift season). Record Store Day (April 19): spike. Back to school (September): moderate lift. Budget accordingly.
LTV reframes CAC. A vinyl customer who buys 3 times over 12 months is worth ~$120 net to Fred's. $25 CAC yields 4.8x LTV:CAC. Don't optimize for first order — optimize for list captures and repeat signals.
TikTok: brand not revenue. CPM $3–8, lower purchase intent. Organic TikTok (bin pull videos, Murray's personality) does more than paid here. TikTok Shop integration is Phase 2.
Instagram Shop sync. Tag records directly in Instagram posts. When Murray posts a record, the product page is one tap away. Zero incremental effort once configured.

Oversights from Full 43-Report Audit — Not Captured Elsewhere
Shopify Advanced = $299/month, not optional. Non-negotiable for Carrier Calculated Shipping API, granular reporting, reduced transaction fees. Total SaaS burn ~$688 CAD/month. Break-even: 46 records at $75 avg. Be explicit with Jim on ongoing cost vs. benefit.
Shopify's 3-option variant limit is a hard wall. Report 18: Used vinyl needs 5 attributes (Format, Country, Media Grade, Sleeve Grade, Matrix). Must use Product Options middleware app (Easify or Hulk). Grail pressings $100+ must be standalone listings — not variants — for SEO and photography.
Zero-Damage Guarantee needs formal SOP + marketing copy. Report 30: Ship vinyl outside its sleeve (vinyl in inner sleeve, decoupled from jacket). Add 12x12 cardboard stiffener pads. Whiplash/Mighty Mailer cruciform mailers. Shipping Policy page must include diagram of this packing method — converts anxious high-ticket buyers.
RSD bot defense must be planned months in advance. Report 23: Bots hit /cart/add.js backend directly — bypassing the storefront. Defenses: CrowdHandler virtual queue (intercepts before Shopify), hCaptcha on checkout, invisible honeypot fields (bots fill them, Shopify Flow auto-cancels order), disable guest checkout for RSD items entirely.
Audio previews are a conversion lever, not a feature add-on. Report 27: 30-sec AAC/256kbps previews hosted on dedicated CDN (not Shopify native). JS must be deferred so it doesn't kill LCP. Sticky waveform player persists as user scrolls. Critical for Fred's unreleased Newfoundland music section — only way to let diaspora customers hear local artists before buying.
Friday Drop Content Factory SOP — define the shoot day now. Report 28: 1 hour every Thursday, filmed on smartphone. Three templates: (1) POV box-cutter unboxing, (2) Tony Staff Autopsy (points to runout groove, explains pressing value), (3) Reel background audio = top-streamed song from featured record. CTA must name exact time: "Friday Drop goes live 10 AM NT."
"The Back Room" $99/year VIP subscription is unbuilt. Report 36: Non-discount, access-only architecture. $99/year grants: 15-min early SMS before Friday Drop goes public, waitlist override for allocated releases, email concierge for sourcing specific pressings. Managed via Shopify tags + ReCharge. High-LTV collectors pay for friction removal — creates recurring revenue at near-zero delivery cost.
Tourist "Newfoundland Hangover" Klaviyo flow. Report 34 + 09: Segment by billing postal code — if Out-of-Province Tourist, trigger flow. Day 3: Tony thank-you + Spotify playlist of local NL artists. Day 17: "Missing St. John's? We ship global." A $30 in-store tourist transaction has $300 LTV potential if converted to online subscriber — currently zero of these are captured.
Staff adoption: reverse-mentorship, not training sessions. Report 29: No Saturday Powerpoints. Pair youngest staff with Tony — junior clicks, Tony dictates grading + pricing. First show Tony a magic trick: customer asks for a record, staff scans barcode, iPad shows their last 5 purchases + taste profile. Buy-in happens when the tool makes Tony look omniscient, not replaceable.

"Grade G" → immediate termination. LoopOS/Reusely handles auto reverse-shipping labels + store credit payouts. Payouts in Shopify gift cards keeps money in ecosystem.
