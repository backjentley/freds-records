# Product Requirements Document (PRD): Fred's Records Digital E-Commerce Platform

## 1. Executive Summary
**Client:** Fred's Records (St. John's, Newfoundland) - Canada's oldest independent record store (54-year legacy).
**Stakeholders:** Murr Mo Savich, two siblings, and Uncle Jim (Majority Shareholder, 76 years old).
**Objective:** Transforming a legendary brick-and-mortar record shop into a global e-commerce brand ("The Digital Cratedigger" experience) without sacrificing its soul, history, or community magic.
**Core Proposition:** A performance-based partnership model (profit share) with minimal upfront cost to alleviate risk for Uncle Jim, modernizing their sales funnel to capture the massive untapped online market.

## 2. Business Goals & Opportunity
- **Current State:** The store generates ~$1M/year exclusively through in-person sales. Online sales are non-existent. The current website is completely outdated (used locally as an example of "what not to do").
- **Revenue Targets:** An immediate baseline increase of 15-25% in annual sales, with long-term potential to reach multi-million online revenue.
- **Inventory Turnaround:** Solve the storage bottleneck for highly profitable used record buyouts (75-90% margin) by rapidly moving inventory online.
- **Merchandising:** Tap into the massive demand for Fred's Records apparel worldwide, moving from local hand-deliveries to scalable e-commerce.

## 3. Target Audience
- **The Veterans:** Older audiophiles and dedicated record collectors who value authenticity, proper hardware, and deep cuts.
- **The New Generation:** Younger demographics (18-27) driving the resurgence of vinyl (e.g., Tyler the Creator, Fleetwood Mac), who view records as premium collectibles.
- **Ex-Pats & Tourists:** People across Canada and globally who have visited Fred's Records and want to support the legacy from afar.

## 4. Design & UX Philosophy (Critical Constraints)
- **Authenticity First:** Murr is strictly opposed to "AI slop" or lazy, generic e-commerce templates. The design must be bespoke, premium, and dripping with character.
- **Storytelling:** The UI must center around the legacy of 198 Duckworth Street. Heavy use of high-res historical imagery, the iconic headphones logo, and tales of the founders (Fred and Jim).
- **Vibe:** "The Digital Cratedigger." It should feel like stepping into a legendary indie record shop—warm, deeply curated, and artistic. Look to stores like Sonic Boom for inspiration (art-centric, credible).

## 5. Core Features & Requirements

### 5.1. E-Commerce & Inventory Sync
- **Unified Catalog:** Ability to list both new drops and the vast used record collection.
- **Intelligent Back-end / Discogs Integration:** A system that tracks what is selling well in the market and potentially syncs with Discogs logic to automate pricing and identification.
- **Idiot-Proof Fulfillment System:** The backend must be frictionless for older, long-tenured employees. It should be as simple as printing a label and shipping, tracking inventory accurately so in-store staff aren't confused by online buys.

### 5.2. Merchandising & High-Ticket Accessories
- **Apparel Store:** A dedicated section for Fred's Records merch (hoodies, t-shirts, etc.). Must accommodate both bulk stock and potential print-on-demand for trendy items.
- **Hardware Integration:** Introduce high-margin, professional record players (e.g., Pro-Ject, $500+) and audio care accessories to capture higher AOV. 

### 5.3. Frontend Experience
- **Advanced Search & Filtering:** Users must seamlessly sort by genre, new/used, artist, and "Fresh in the Bins."
- **Legacy Page:** A dedicated about page that sells the history—photos of Fred, the store in the 70s/80s, and the community impact.
- **Mobile Optimized:** Must flawlessy serve the younger demographic ordering from their phones.

## 6. Phased Rollout Strategy
- **Phase 1 (The Pitch Showcase):** A high-fidelity conceptual prototype to win over Uncle Jim. Focus on the visual storytelling, legacy integration, and the seamless user interface. Must demonstrate the "first online sale" magic.
- **Phase 2 (MVP Launch):** Launch the website with a curated selection of 20-50 high-impact "Drop" items (rare used, sealed new, and fresh merch) to establish proof of concept and start the profit-share flywheel.
- **Phase 3 (Scaling):** Full inventory integration, ad campaigns, and exploring centralized fulfillment to reduce shipping costs and overhead.

## 7. Risks & Mitigation
- **Stakeholder Hesitation:** Uncle Jim is protective. *Mitigation:* Present a risk-free, performance-based payment structure. Do not disrupt current in-store operations.
- **Design Rejection:** The aesthetic must not feel corporate. *Mitigation:* Collaborate closely with Murr, leveraging his artistic background and ensuring all assets are authentic to Fred's.
- **Inventory Discrepancy:** Selling a record online that just sold in-store. *Mitigation:* Implement a real-time POS to E-Comm sync system.
